The thing worth internalizing

Sovrn needs clicks, not sales. Amazon needs 3 qualifying sales in 180 days or it closes your account. These are different gates with different clocks — don't conflate them. And never ask anyone to click your links to "help" — affiliate networks read coordinated clicking as invalid traffic and ban for it. Real people using the quiz for real is both safe and the only thing that works.

The call we made

No model. The clothes are the content.

Two Runway rounds and roughly 64 credits produced a woman who still read about thirty, with waxy skin and no flyaways. It looked AI, and everyone in your audience would have known.

That was worth walking away from for two reasons. The persona was chosen for the style-over-40 wedge — an underserved, high-intent niche — and a face that reads thirty puts you back in the most crowded market there is. And your whole promise is "this could be you." A synthetic face quietly undercuts that at the exact moment the quiz asks for an email.

What replaces her

Flat-lays and capsule grids — the neutral, labelled "one capsule / X ways" layouts that fill the Pinterest board you already said you liked. They cost nothing, they can't look AI because they're real product photos, they show the product actually doing its job, and they're the format Pinterest genuinely saves.

Elle survives as a voice — the warm big-sister tone in the captions. She just doesn't need a face.

The one blocker

Eight product photos

About ten minutes of your time, and it unlocks every visual asset in this kit. Amazon requires product images come through SiteStripe, so this one genuinely can't be automated.

  1. Turn on SiteStripe — the grey bar at the top of Amazon when you're logged into Associates
  2. Open each search below and pick a piece you'd actually recommend
  3. SiteStripe → Text → copy the short link (it already carries your tag). Paste into a note.
  4. Right-click the product photo → Save Image As → into capsule-grid/
Pick well

Choose products shot on a plain white background — they drop straight into the cream tiles and read editorial. Skip anything photographed on a model; it breaks the flat-lay look.

What she posts

Four content pillars

Every post is one of these four. Rotate them. Don't invent a fifth until one of these proves itself.

Closet chaos → capsule
The transformation hook. The emotional entry point. Everyone recognizes the full-closet-nothing-to-wear feeling — that recognition is the whole ad.
One capsule, five ways
The save-worthy one. Proves the product's core claim: fewer pieces, more outfits. This is the format that gets bookmarked and re-shared.
Shop it at every budget
The differentiator. Markets your budget-tier toggle directly. Same look at Value, Everyday, Elevated — nobody else is doing this.
The 3-piece outfit formula
The teaching one. Pure utility, no selling. Builds the authority that makes the other three land.

Thirty days

What to expect — honestly

The goal of month one is not money. It's to go live, learn what lands, and build the first audience. A cold-start first sale realistically arrives on day 45–90. That's normal. It is not failure.

WeekDoExpect
1 Accounts live, bios + link set. 3–4 posts, cross-posted to all three. First views. A handful of followers. Zero sales.
2 4 posts. Vary the hook every time. Watch which one moves. Hundreds to a few thousand views. First link clicks trickle in.
3 Take the best performer and make three more like it. Email your first leads. One post may pop. Tens of clicks. First quiz completions. A sale is a bonus.
4 4 posts, keep the winning format. Review the numbers. Compounding reach. Real email list. Maybe a first sale.
The one rule

Make once, post to all three. Four posts a week for four weeks, with one that lands, beats a burst followed by silence. The algorithm rewards steady. One reel hitting can change everything — but only if you're still feeding it when it does.
